What I've been up to lately...

Some fun side-projects and extracurriculars

WCS Explorations

As part of our work as co-chairs of WCS Explorations, my co-chair and I have been working on creating basic tutorials for a variety of technical tools, a book club to encourage more consideration of ethics in computer science, and coordinating project teams to help underclassmen gain project experience. Check out our workshop slides and resources on the Explorations webpage!

Explorations side projects

I get really easily excited about new things, so I've had the opportunity to develop new resources for WCS members (separate from our Explorations workshops) and improve my web dev skills at the same time. Check out our collaborative MBTI board for WCS members and our basic binary explainer!

The Percentage Project

On March 31, 2021, The Percentage Project launched the 2021 social media campaign. The campaign included 11 schools, including (for the first time!) the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, thanks to the efforts of our awesome team of 5! Earlier in the school year, we surveyed ~100 students about their experiences as students at UIUC. We then put a face to the statistic by encouraging students to pick a statistic that means a lot to them and taking a photo with it.

CS Mental Health Committee

As the events chair of the committee, I've been lucky to be able to work on a variety of events to raise awareness for mental health and related advocacy initiatives through events. We've held events such as a panel on mental health during the pandemic and de-stressing nights. Our next big initiative is to plan an engineering mental health conference! It's still a filler site for now, but stay tuned to our website for more details.

Girls Who Code

I'm really lucky to help teach workshops to a group of grade 3 through 12 girls! Last semester, I led a workshop about data visualization and this semester I'm helping with an artificial intelligence workshop.

Research

I've been really fortunate to be participating in research this semester. By coincidence, both projects are at the intersection of crop science and computer science, but I've really enjoyed my experience so far.

Nationwide Crop Classification from Satellite Imagery

This semester, I am a SPIN research intern at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications. My task for the semester is to determine which crops are growing where based on satellite imagery taken at a variety of different resolutions.

Cancer Gene Expression

In fall of 2018, I was a PURE undergraduate researcher working with a mentor on using non-negative matrix factorization to identify cancers from their gene expressions. The code and poster can be found in this GitHub Repo.

Academia Sinica

In summer 2018, I was a research intern at the Academia Sinica Institute of Information Sciences. I was primarily reading research papers about computer vision and applications in a wide variety of fields.

Crop Heritability Prediction

This semester, I am also an undergraduate researcher helping with a project that seeks to predict the heritability of certain traits for crops. I've been helping with calculating ground truth data and baseline metrics for the pipeline.

Imputation and Missingness in Neural Networks

The spring and summer after my PURE project, I continued working with my mentor on learning about missing data in neural networks. I performed a literature survey and started implementing a neural network that used informative missingness, the idea that missing data has a helpful pattern that can be used to help classify data.

2019-2020

HackIllinois 2020 Co-director In February 2020, right before the pandemic hit, I was one of the co-directors of the premiere open source hackathon. With a team of 50, we hosted 1000 people (800 attendees, 35 mentors, 17 companies, and 200 volunteers) for 36 hours. They worked on a variety of open source projects that are used by thousands from around the world. It was an incredible experience to work with so many talented people to host one of the last large events for the university before social distancing began. More information about the event is available at our website. I also had a chance to get back into writing through the organization's blog. I wrote a short piece about the history of HackIllinois.

Earlier this year

Facebook Software Engineering Intern Over the summer of 2020, I was a software engineering intern in the privacy organization. My project was to develop a new framework to test feature extractors used to detect user-identifiable information. My system decreased experiment runtime by half and created an intuitive UI to expedite feature extractor development.
