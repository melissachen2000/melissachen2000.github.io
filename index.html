Hi! My name is Melissa Chen.

I am a student at the University of Illinois studying computer science.

At Illinois, I co-direct HackIllinois, a 36-hour open source hackathon, and co-chair ACM's SIGMusic, a special interest group on computational music. I'm also involved in planning the Reflections | Projections tech conference and staff CS 196, the university's freshman honors course.

This summer, I'm doing research on missing features in neural networks, a continuation of my research during the past school year on unsupervised learning and imputation methods. Outside of classes, I love listening to music, reading books, and discovering neighborhood coffeeshops.

My recent work:

HackIllinois: A Journey discusses the trials and successes of HackIllinois staffs from 2014 to 2019 and looks forward to the planning process for HackIllinois 2020.

Cancer Gene Expressions and NMF uses non-negative matrix factorization to analyze cancer gene expressions.

Safeways plots the safest route between two points on campus, avoiding recent and frequent crime locations.
