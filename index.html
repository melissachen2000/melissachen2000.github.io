About me

Hi! My name is Melissa Chen. I am a student at the University of Illinois studying computer science.

One of my greatest passions is figuring out where data can be condensed into something that is easier to handle and understand. In many of my projects, I have worked as a data scientist or a data-oriented software engineer. The past two summers, I have been researching AI; this summer I was working on how to interpret and handle data with missing values within a neural network, a continuation of my research over the past school year on unsupervised learning and imputation methods.

At Illinois, I co-direct HackIllinois, a 36-hour open source hackathon, and co-chair ACM's SIGMusic, a special interest group on computational music. I'm also involved in planning the Reflections | Projections tech conference and staff CS 196, the university's freshman honors course.

Outside of classes, I love listening to music, reading mystery novels, and discovering neighborhood coffeeshops. My recent reads include Agatha Christie's And Then There Were None and Robert Thorogood's novelizations of the Death in Paradise TV series.
